Ultrasound modes

Created At：2022-03-25 10:38 Updated At：2023-04-19 19:12

M-mode takes a single, vertical segment and creates a 2D tracing of movement over time. The y-axis aligns with points along the vertical segment, and the x-axis measures time. Thus, the graph plots changes in depths of echo-producing interfaces per unit of time, representing movement.

Color-flow Doppler mode shows directional flow qualitatively. With the color map shown, red represents flow towards the transducer, and blue is flow away from the transducer.

Power-flow Doppler shows flow but does not indicate direction. It is very sensitive and can be used for identifying perfusion in tissue that has a low perfusion state, such as the testicles. Alternatively, it can be used to detect tissue movement, such as in this clip of lung sliding.

Pulsed-wave Doppler shows two images simultaneously on the monitor. At the top is a sampling gate, and at the bottom are flow waveforms with velocity represented as the height of the waveform.
