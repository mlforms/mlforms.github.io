GI Bleed Balloon Tamponade

Created At：2024-05-03 11:02 Updated At：2024-05-05 18:18
  1. Test both balloon cuffs for leaks
  2. Tie/suture an NGT to Blakemore, terminating 3cm proximal to esophagus
  3. Place Blakemore, inflate gastric balloon w 50cc, clamp port, and XR confirm placement. If placement good, attach manometer with Y connector and inflate to 15mmHg then clamp port and detach
  4. Attach traction to Blakemore tubing with 500cc or 1L saline bag pulley w Curlex
  5. Suction gastric and NG tube to look for blood
  6. If blood in NGT, inflate esophageal balloon to 35-40mmHg
