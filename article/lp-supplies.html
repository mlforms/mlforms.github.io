LP supplies

Created At：2023-02-10 07:16 Updated At：2024-04-08 17:53
  • Skin marker/syringe
  • Chlorhex swabs
  • Longer LP needle 20"
  • 10mL 2% lidocaine
  • 18" & 25" needles and 6cc syringe
  • 1mg Versed
  • Extra OR towels
  • Silk tape
  • Gown/bouffant
  • Mayo stand in room
  • Sterile gloves
  • LP kit
  • Take off badge

Prior to LP check:
[] platelets (≥100k definitely fine, even ≥50k is probably fine)
[] coags (INR ≤ 1.6)
[] head CT(sometimes)
[] h/o plavix or AC use?

Steps

  • Get patient positioned with chucks underneath and sheets out of the way
  • shift clothes so pt doesn't get betadine on them
  • Tape gown/strings out of the way
  • palpate/US for landmarks and mark the site with syringe suction/marker
  • open the kit sterilely on mayo stand
  • toss 6cc syringes, 18 & 25 gauge needles onto field
  • don gown and sterile gloves
  • have nurse hold the lidocaine to draw sterilely
  • apply betadine in large area
  • sterilely drape pt
  • inject lidocaine at the site
  • set up manometer and tubing
  • set up collection tubes and have them close so it's easy to reach for them
  • have somewhere to set aside inner stylet
  • hold needle w bevel pointing laterally

Feedback:
4/1: - used ~5-7cc 2% lidocaine and 1mg versed and pt said it was very tolerable

  • having pt sit up threw me off but did get it on first attempt

With paramedian, you don't have to poke around up, down, left, right. You only have up-down axis to deal with.

Paramedian angle may be less likely to cause post-LP HA as your tunnel a little bit instead of directly poking.
